Sound Circuits

Sound Circuits is a bi-weekly meetup for you to come work on your DIY music projects. It’s easier to make progress on side projects if you have a structured time to show up and work on them. We have extra tools, space to work, and like-minded people you can hang out with and learn from.

You can work on any music-related project you want here. Making pedals, fixing equipment, building synthesizers, programming Arduinos to make sound, soldering cables, designing a new instrument from the ground up–these are just a few examples of great things to do here.

We welcome everyone, regardless of gender, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, or religion.

What equipment is already there?

We encourage you to please bring your own soldering iron, flush cutters, and solder if you have it–just throw it in your bag on the way over. However, we have a collection of tools that people are welcome to use as well:

  • 2x Large spools of solder (63/37)
  • 3x gold braid tip cleaners
  • 3x Weller soldering stations
  • 1x Hakko 808 desoldering vacuum gun
  • 1x tip set for the soldering stations
  • 5x 3M respirator masks
  • 3x Hakko ESD-safe smoke absorbers
  • 2x multimeters
  • 1x oscilloscope
  • 3x flush cutters
  • 1x precision tweezers (can be used for SMD)
  • 1x Bag of alligator clips
  • 5x Spools of wire

As well, there is a SMD hot air rework station that can be brought on request–email root@soundcircuits.org.

I need a project!

Here are some places where you can buy DIY kits/projects to work on. The list is loosely ordered from cheapest to most expensive. Price ranges given don’t necessarily include ordering the electronic parts, although some kits already have them all included.

DIYRE

DIYRE sells really useful DIY recording kits. Everyone needs some DI boxes - that’s a cheap and simple project to start with. They also have cool Colour 500-series modules that you can use for adding vibe to your tracks.

Build Your Own Clone

BYOC is a classic spot to pick up effects pedal kits for cheap. They are relatively simple to build, and sound great. Almost any effect you can think of, they probably have a kit for it. They also have good sales to watch out for. Their Confidence Booster kit is only $15, and is a perfect intro to soldering.

Arduino

Pick yourself up an Arduino, and start learning to program and build hardware. There are a ton of music projects that you can follow to teach yourself more and more skills.

Music From Outer Space

Ray Wilson is a legendary figure in the DIY synth world, who published tons of designs and projects on his Music From Outer Space website and has educated folks for decades. Unfortunately, he passed away from cancer in 2016, but Synthcube has taken over distributing his kits in his absence. There are single-board analog synths in the $30-60 range, weird noise boxes, vocoders, and full modular synth designs.

Old Crow (CS80)

Old Crow aka Scott Rider is a synth/electronics expert who started selling clone kits of classic synthesizers. Check out the Crowminius, a Minimoog clone that fits in a small attache case. For the truly bold, build an 8-voice Oberheim OB-X rackmount clone. The PCB boards seem pretty cheap, but research the part cost first!

E-licktronic

These folks sell full kits to build a clone of the Roland TR-808 or TR-909. Rad.

Hairball Audio

Local to Seattle, they sell 500 series and rackmount preamp and compressor kits. They have many flavors of UREI 1176 clones, as well as their own beautiful sounding Lola preamps.
